INTRODUCTION

Agriculture remains the backbone of India’s economy, with a large section of the population dependent on farming for their livelihood. To support the welfare of farmers, the Government of Andhra Pradesh introduced the Raithu Bharosa Scheme, a flagship program aimed at providing financial assistance, input subsidies, and support services to small and marginal farmers.

Effective implementation of this scheme requires a robust and transparent system for identifying eligible beneficiaries. Eligibility is typically determined based on criteria such as land ownership, crop cultivation patterns, socio-economic background, and exclusion of ineligible categories like government employees or high-income individuals.

The identification process plays a crucial role in ensuring that benefits reach the rightful recipients and that resources are utilized efficiently.

This study focuses on developing and refining mechanisms for accurately identifying eligible farmers through the use of land records, digital databases, and field verification. By addressing challenges such as data inconsistencies, fraudulent claims, and lack of awareness, the goal is to support inclusive and efficient delivery of benefits under the Raithu Bharosa Scheme, ultimately contributing to improved agricultural productivity and rural development.
